Recent Success Stories

Aurora the Eagle-Owl

Rescue Date: March 15, 2024

Condition: Broken wing, dehydration

Story: Aurora was found by a hiker in Table Mountain National Park. She had a compound fracture in her left wing and was unable to fly. After surgery and three months of rehabilitation, Aurora made a full recovery and was successfully released back into her territory.

Release Date: June 20, 2024

Pumba the Porcupine

Rescue Date: January 10, 2024

Condition: Orphaned baby, malnutrition

Story: Pumba was found alone after his mother was unfortunately hit by a car. He was only two weeks old and required round-the-clock care. After six months of rehabilitation, Pumba was strong enough to be released into a protected area.

Found Injured Wildlife? Here's What to Do

Step 1: Assess the Situation

Keep a safe distance. Do not approach dangerous animals like baboons, snakes, or large carnivores. Observe from a distance to determine if the animal is truly injured or just resting.

Step 2: Keep the Animal Calm

Keep noise to a minimum and avoid sudden movements. Keep children and pets away from the area. Do not attempt to feed the animal.

Step 3: Contact Us Immediately

Call our emergency hotline: 081 234 5678. Be ready to provide:

  • Your exact location
  • Type of animal (if known)
  • Animal's condition
  • Any visible injuries

Step 4: Secure the Area (If Safe)

If it's safe to do so, you can prevent the animal from wandering into traffic by creating a gentle barrier. Never put yourself in danger.

Step 5: Wait for Our Team

Our trained rescuers will arrive as quickly as possible. Continue to monitor the animal from a safe distance until help arrives.

Important Notes:

  • Do not attempt to handle the animal yourself
  • Never give food or water to injured wildlife
  • Keep the animal in a quiet, dark place if you must contain it temporarily
  • Always prioritize your own safety
